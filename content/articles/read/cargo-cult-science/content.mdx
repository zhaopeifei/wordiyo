During the Middle Ages there were all kinds of crazy ideas, such as that a piece of rhinoceros horn would increase potency. Then a method was discovered for separating the ideas — which was to try one to see if it worked, and if it didn't work, to eliminate it. This method became organized, of course, into science. And it developed very well, so that we are now in the scientific age. It is such a scientific age, in fact, that we have difficulty in understanding how witch doctors could ever have existed, when nothing that they proposed ever really worked — or very little of it did.

<Trans lang="zh">在中世纪，人们有各种各样的荒诞想法，比如犀牛角可以增强体力。后来人们发现了一种筛选想法的方法——试一试看它是否有效，如果无效就淘汰掉。这种方法逐渐被组织成科学体系。它发展得非常好，以至于我们现在身处科学时代。事实上，这是一个如此"科学"的时代，我们甚至很难理解巫医怎么可能存在过——因为他们提出的东西几乎没有一样真正有效。</Trans>

## The South Seas Example

But even today I meet lots of people who sooner or later get me into a conversation about UFOs, or astrology, or some form of mysticism, expanded consciousness, new types of awareness, ESP, and so forth. And I've concluded that it's not a scientific world.

<Trans lang="zh">但即使在今天，我还是会遇到很多人，他们迟早会把话题引到 UFO、占星术、某种形式的神秘主义、扩展意识、新型感知、超感官知觉等等。我的结论是：这并不是一个科学的世界。</Trans>

Most people believe so many wonderful things that I decided to investigate why they did. And what has been referred to as my curiosity for investigation has landed me in a difficulty where I found so much junk to talk about that I can't do it in this talk. I'm going to be particular about only one little thing — a thing that is not associated with, but is analogous to, a kind of cargo cult.

<Trans lang="zh">大多数人相信那么多奇妙的事情，我决定调查一下为什么。而我这种被称为"好奇心"的调查癖给我带来了一个麻烦——我发现有太多垃圾可以谈论，一场演讲根本讲不完。所以我只打算专门讲一件小事——一件与草包族崇拜无关、但有类比意义的事情。</Trans>

In the South Seas there is a cargo cult of people. During the war they saw airplanes land with lots of good materials, and they want the same thing to happen now. So they've arranged to make things like runways, to put fires along the sides of the runways, to make a wooden hut for a man to sit in, with two wooden pieces on his head like headphones and bars of bamboo sticking out like antennas — he's the controller — and they wait for the airplanes to land. They're doing everything right. The form is perfect. It looks exactly the way it looked before. But it doesn't work. No airplanes land. So I call these things cargo cult science, because they follow all the apparent precepts and forms of scientific investigation, but they're missing something essential, because the planes don't land.

<Trans lang="zh">在南太平洋岛屿上有一种"货物崇拜"。战争期间，当地人看到飞机降落并带来大量好东西，他们希望同样的事情再次发生。于是他们造了跑道一样的东西，在跑道两侧点上火把，建了一个木屋让一个人坐在里面，头上戴着两块像耳机一样的木头，还有竹竿伸出来像天线——他就是"控制员"——然后他们等待飞机降落。他们每件事都做得有模有样。形式完美无缺。看起来跟以前一模一样。但它不起作用。飞机不会降落。所以我把这些东西叫做"草包族科学"，因为它们遵循了科学研究所有表面上的规则和形式，但缺少了某种本质的东西，因为飞机就是不降落。</Trans>

## What's Missing

Now it behooves me, of course, to tell you what they're missing. But it would be just about as difficult to explain to the South Sea Islanders how they have to arrange things so that they get some wealth in their system. It is not something simple like telling them how to improve the shapes of the earphones. But there is one feature I notice that is generally missing in cargo cult science. That is the idea that we all hope you have learned in studying science in school — we never explicitly say what this is, but just hope that you catch on by all the examples of scientific investigation. It is interesting, therefore, to bring it out now and speak of it explicitly.

<Trans lang="zh">当然，我有义务告诉你们他们缺少的是什么。但要向南海岛民解释他们该如何安排才能让系统运转起来，也同样困难。这不是像告诉他们如何改进耳机形状那么简单的事。但有一个特征，我注意到在草包族科学中普遍缺失。那就是一个我们都希望你们在学校学科学时已经领悟到的理念——我们从不明确地说出这是什么，而只是希望你们通过一个个科学研究的范例自己领会。因此，现在把它明确提出来谈一谈是很有意义的。</Trans>

It's a kind of scientific integrity, a principle of scientific thought that corresponds to a kind of utter honesty — a kind of leaning over backwards. For example, if you're doing an experiment, you should report everything that you think might make it invalid — not only what you think is right about it: other causes that could possibly explain your results; and things you thought of that you've eliminated by some other experiment, and how they worked — to make sure the other fellow can tell they have been eliminated.

<Trans lang="zh">那是一种科学诚信，一种科学思维的原则，对应着一种彻底的诚实——一种宁可委屈自己也要保持公正的态度。比如，如果你在做实验，你应该报告所有你认为可能使结果无效的因素——不仅仅是你认为正确的部分：还有可能解释你结果的其他原因；你想到过但通过其他实验排除了的因素，以及那些实验是如何运作的——以确保别人能判断这些因素确实被排除了。</Trans>

## The First Principle

Details that could throw doubt on your interpretation must be given, if you know them. You must do the best you can — if you know anything at all wrong, or possibly wrong — to explain it. If you make a theory, for example, and advertise it, or put it out, then you must also put down all the facts that disagree with it, as well as those that agree with it.

<Trans lang="zh">如果你知道任何可能对你的解读产生怀疑的细节，你必须给出来。你必须尽最大努力——如果你知道任何错误的、或可能错误的地方——去解释它。如果你提出了一个理论并加以宣传或公布，你必须同时列出所有与它不符的事实，以及与它相符的事实。</Trans>

There is also a more subtle problem. When you have put a lot of ideas together to make an elaborate theory, you want to make sure, when explaining what it fits, that those things it fits are not just the things that gave you the idea for the theory; but that the finished theory makes something else come out right, in addition.

<Trans lang="zh">还有一个更微妙的问题。当你把许多想法组合在一起构建出一个精致的理论时，在解释它能解释什么的时候，你要确保那些被它解释的现象不仅仅是当初启发你提出理论的那些东西——你的完成版理论还必须额外预测出一些新的正确结论。</Trans>

In summary, the idea is to try to give all of the information to help others to judge the value of your contribution; not just the information that leads to judgment in one particular direction or another.

<Trans lang="zh">总而言之，核心理念是尽可能提供所有信息来帮助他人判断你的贡献的价值——而不是只提供那些引导他人得出某个特定结论的信息。</Trans>

## Fooling Yourself

The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool. So you have to be very careful about that. After you've not fooled yourself, it's easy not to fool other scientists. You just have to be honest in a conventional way after that.

<Trans lang="zh">第一条原则是你不能欺骗自己——而你恰恰是最容易被欺骗的人。所以你必须在这方面非常小心。在你做到不欺骗自己之后，不欺骗其他科学家就容易了。之后你只需要按照常规方式保持诚实就行。</Trans>

I would like to add something that's not essential to the science, but something I kind of believe, which is that you should not fool the layman when you're talking as a scientist. I am not trying to tell you what to do about cheating on your wife, or fooling your girlfriend, or something like that, when you're not trying to be a scientist, but just trying to be an ordinary human being. We'll leave those problems up to you and your rabbi. I'm talking about a specific, extra type of integrity that is not lying, but bending over backwards to show how you are maybe wrong, that you ought to have when acting as a scientist. And this is our responsibility as scientists, certainly to other scientists, and I think to laymen.

<Trans lang="zh">我想补充一些对科学本身而言不是必需的、但我确实相信的东西，那就是：当你以科学家的身份说话时，你不应该欺骗外行人。我不是要告诉你在不做科学家、只是做普通人的时候，应该怎么处理对妻子不忠或欺骗女朋友之类的事——那些问题留给你和你的心灵导师去解决吧。我说的是一种特定的、额外的诚信——不是说不撒谎，而是竭尽全力去展示你可能是错的——这是你作为科学家时应该具备的品质。这是我们作为科学家的责任，当然是对其他科学家的责任，我认为也是对外行人的责任。</Trans>

## Real-World Examples

For example, I was a little surprised when I was talking to a friend who was going to go on the radio. He does work on cosmology and astronomy, and he wondered how he would explain what the applications of this work were. "Well," I said, "there aren't any." He said, "Yes, but then we won't get support for more research of this kind." I think that's kind of dishonest. If you're representing yourself as a scientist, then you should explain to the layman what you're doing — and if they don't want to support you under those circumstances, then that's their decision.

<Trans lang="zh">举个例子，我有一次跟一个要上广播节目的朋友聊天时有点吃惊。他做宇宙学和天文学研究，正在琢磨该怎么向听众解释他的研究有什么应用。"嗯，"我说，"没有什么应用。"他说："是啊，但那样的话我们就拿不到更多的研究经费了。"我觉得这有点不诚实。如果你以科学家的身份出现，你应该向外行人解释你在做什么——如果他们在了解真相后不想资助你，那是他们的决定。</Trans>

One example of the principle is the following: If you've made up your mind to test a theory, or you want to explain some idea, you should always decide to publish it whichever way it comes out. If we only publish results of a certain kind, we can make the argument look good. We must publish *both* kinds of results.

<Trans lang="zh">这个原则的一个例子如下：如果你决定验证一个理论，或者你想解释某个想法，你应该事先就决定不管结果如何都要发表。如果我们只发表某一类结果，我们可以让论证看起来很完美。我们必须发表*两种*结果。</Trans>

## Scientific Integrity

So I have just one wish for you — the good luck to be somewhere where you are free to maintain the kind of integrity I have described, and where you do not feel forced by a need to maintain your position in the organization, or financial support, or so on, to lose your integrity. May you have that freedom.

<Trans lang="zh">所以我对你们只有一个祝愿——祝你们有好运，能身处一个地方，在那里你们可以自由地保持我所描述的那种诚信，而不会因为维护你们在组织中的地位、或经济来源等需要而被迫丧失诚信。愿你们拥有那份自由。</Trans>
